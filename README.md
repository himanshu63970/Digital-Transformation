# Digital-Transformation
"Digital Transformation ” is an insightful topic that delves into the world of digital transformation and its profound impact on businesses. It provides a comprehensive guide for organizations seeking to adapt and thrive in the digital era.
Digital Transformation

Nowadays there are lots of businesses and industries are there worldwide. Also, we have evolving technologies and quality data, so data transformation is essential. Digital transformation is the process of integration of digital technologies into all the areas of a business, fundamentally changing how we operate and deliver values to customers. In other words, we can say that Adoption and implementation of digital technology by an organization to create new or modify existing products, services, and operations by translating business processes into a digital format.

The role for its integration is to increase value through innovation, invention, improved customer experience and efficiency. Focusing on efficiency and costs. In digital transformation what we are doing is simply converting our day-to-day manual tasks into technical tasks for e.g., before the implementation of technologies we have to write all the records in books or ledgers so there were chances of data loss and leak now due to emerging technologies we can directly note all the records in computers or upload in cloud.

History of Digital Transformation

Digital transformation means turning paper or physical information into digital data using devices like scanners and digital audio recorders. This change has been happening more since the 1990s because more people are using the internet. However, it extends beyond mere digitization, demanding a broader perspective that considers how emerging digital technologies can instigate substantial changes within products, processes, and organizations.

In 2019, a notable review provided an illuminating perspective on digital transformation. It defined digital transformation as a purposeful process geared toward elevating an entity by introducing substantial alterations to its characteristics through the amalgamation of information, computing, communication, and connectivity technologies. Essentially, digital transformation can be viewed as a socio-technical endeavor that harnesses the synergy of these technological components to facilitate meaningful enhancements across diverse domains.

Adopting digital technology can bring benefits to a business, however, some company cultures can struggle with the changes required by digital transformation because it is completely a cultural change.

1.Culture: 

A culture that supports innovation and digital change is created from a blend of capable digital tools, employee support and communication.

It requires changing the hearts, mindsets, and skills of the workforce to support desired culture.

2. Compute:

The umbrella of Digital Transformation encompasses a wide variety of technologies including applications and software such as AI/ML, IOT, Cloud computing, blockchain, cyber security.

AI/ML deals with automation, chatbots, personalization in E-Commerce, robotics etc.
IOT deals with smart home appliances, smart agricultural activities, sensors, CCTV cameras etc.
Blockchain deals with providing safe and secure money related transactions.
Cloud computing deals with online data storage, backup and recovery, big data analysis etc.
Cyber Security deals with network security surveillance, software security, data privacy etc. 
3. Communication:

It is an essential pillar of digital transformation without which we cannot express our views to others. Without proper communication, a business can take longer than anticipated due to communication gaps and misunderstandings which results in higher costs.

Communication facilitates participation. It is the use of online tools like E-Mails, Social media messaging and texting to reach other individuals or a specific audience to share a message or group of messages.

4.Control:

It deals with customer feedback and cyber security. It reveals the essence of system-based processes, enabling enterprises to trace problems in business management and take actions based on facts. The three core goals of this transformation are: increased transparency, smarter insights, and enabling process optimization.

Methods, policies, and organizational procedures that ensure the safety of an organization’s assets, accuracy and reliability of its accounting records and operational adherence to management standards.

5. Continuity:

It includes business sustainability. Business continuity is the strategic and tactical capabilities of the organization to plan for and respond to incidents and business disruptions in order to continue business operations at an acceptable predefined level.

Important aspects in digital transformation

1. Blockchain: Blockchain is an innovative and decentralized digital ledger technology specifically crafted to record transactions securely across a network of computers. Its primary objectives are to guarantee data security, transparency, and immutability. Originally developed as the foundational technology for cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, blockchain has now expanded its utility to various other domains and industries, offering transformative solutions beyond digital currencies.

2. Cloud computing: Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics and intelligence over the internet to offer faster innovation, flexible resources and economies of scale. In Cloud computing we store and access the data on remote servers that are situated on the internet rather than in the computer’s hard drive or local server. 

Benefits of cloud computing

Cost-It eliminates cost of buying Hardwares to store data.
Speed-It is provided with self service and on demand, so even vast amounts of computing resources can be provisioned in minutes.
Scalability-Storage can be increased or decreased according to usage.
Productivity-It removes hardware setup, software patching and other time-consuming tasks.
Performance-Runs on a worldwide network of secured data centers which are regularly upgraded.
Reliability-Data backup and recovery
